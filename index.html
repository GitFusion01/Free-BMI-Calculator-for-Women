What Is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a health indicator that compares your body weight with your height. Doctors and health professionals often use BMI to assess whether a person’s weight falls within a healthy range.

Although BMI does not directly measure body fat, it is widely used because it provides a quick estimate of potential health risks related to weight.

Why BMI Is Important for Women

Maintaining a healthy BMI is important for women because weight balance plays a role in hormonal health, metabolism, and overall well-being.

Women with a healthy BMI generally have a lower risk of conditions such as:

  • Heart disease
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • High blood pressure
  • Hormonal imbalance
  • Metabolic disorders

Limitations of BMI

While BMI is useful, it does have some limitations. It does not account for:

  • Muscle mass
  • Body fat distribution
  • Bone density
  • Age differences

Because of this, BMI should be used as a general guideline rather than a medical diagnosis.
